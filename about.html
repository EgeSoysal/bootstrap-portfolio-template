ABOUT ME

I'm Ege Soysal. Since my childhood, computers and cameras have been a natural part of my life. This early connection with technology evolved over time into a desire to become a creator, rather than just a consumer. From a young age, I strived to be self-taught, to experiment, and to produce tangible work. During this process, I focused on developing myself in the fields of software, technology, and digital production. I worked on projects across different disciplines; some were completed, while others provided me with valuable experience. At this point in my journey, I believe that producing and learning are inseparable. Therefore, my focus remains on evolving, doing better, and pushing my boundaries. Currently, I am pursuing an associate degree in Computer Programming. I make a conscious effort to support my academic education with independent studies. I develop small-scale console projects with the programming languages I learn, sharing them on GitHub to both discipline myself and document my progress. In addition, I conduct independent research on machine learning, data science, and large language models (LLMs). Instead of settling for superficial knowledge in these fields, I strive to understand their underlying logic and think through real-world use cases. I believe that artificial intelligence is not just a trend, but a long-term transformation, and I aim to be a part of it. I spend most of my time studying, learning, and producing. I prioritize building a rock-solid foundation for the long run over short-term results. My goal is to deepen my expertise in technology, software, and AI to develop value-driven projects and make them sustainable.

VISION

During my high school years, I developed a deep interest not only in technical fields, but also in philosophy, different cultures, peoples, and thought systems. During the same period, I conducted research on astronomy, reflecting on the structure of the universe, the concept of time, and human existence within this vast system. Both philosophy and astronomy showed me how small human beings are, yet what immense potential they possess. Studying diverse cultures, belief systems, and lifestyles, combined with my interest in astronomy, allowed me to view the universe from a broader perspective. Throughout this process, I realized that clinging to a single truth or viewpoint limits a person. I came to believe that the meaning of life is to strive to realize the absolute peak of one's potential, regardless of the circumstances. For me, this philosophy means evolving across multiple disciplines rather than advancing in just one, and bringing my diverse knowledge together to create tangible products. I believe knowledge only gains value when converted into production, and production multiplies only when shared. Therefore, I highly value ensuring that the projects I develop, the ideas I put forward, and the work I do contribute not only to me but to others as well. My vision is to deepen my expertise in my fields of interest especially technology, software, and artificial intelligence—while merging this knowledge with a broader intellectual framework. Driven by the curiosity of trying to understand the universe, I aim to connect the philosophical perspective of understanding humanity and society with technical production. By doing so, I want to build works that are not just functional systems, but meaningful creations. The decisions I make, my goals, and my overall outlook on life are built upon this foundation. I prioritize constructing a sustainable, long-term culture of growth and continuous learning over short-term achievements. I am fully aware that I will always need new ideas, different perspectives, and diverse people along this path. Because I believe true growth stems from curiosity, questioning, and sharing. My ultimate vision is built upon creating a life that continuously produces, stays open to learning, and pushes the very boundaries of my potential.

ART

For me, art is not merely about producing aesthetics or making things look 'beautiful.' Art is a way of bringing thought, knowledge, and production together to form a meaningful whole. This is why I ensure that everything I create, regardless of the field it originates from, establishes a connection with art. Software, technology, artificial intelligence, or data are, in themselves, mediums of expression for me. In my production process, what that creation conveys and evokes is just as decisive as technical proficiency. I believe that a finished work should not just function; it must carry a thought, ask a question, and leave an impression on the viewer or user. Therefore, I position my creations not as purely functional products, but as works that possess a narrative, a context, and room for interpretation. I do not view art as a domain separate from the rest of life. On the contrary, I consciously choose to let every work I do touch upon art. In an era where the boundaries between technology and art are increasingly blurred, I believe that intertwining these two fields, rather than tearing them apart, yields more meaningful and powerful results. In my understanding of production, art is experimental, inquisitive, and open ended. It values asking the right questions over providing neat answers. This approach drives me to draw inspiration from different disciplines, step outside conventional boundaries, and seek new forms of expression. Every project is approached as a space for exploration, both technically and artistically. No matter what I produce, I consider preserving this bond with art as a core principle. Because for me, true value lies not just in creating something, but in blending that creation with meaning, emotion, and thought. Art is the common language and the unchanging reference point of all my productions.

FUTURE

After completing high school, I took a gap year to prepare for the university entrance exam. Due to a severe migraine attack during the most critical moment of the exam, I was unable to replicate the high rankings I consistently achieved in the nationwide practice exams, which led me to enroll in an associate degree program. However, I view this phase not as a setback, but as an opportunity to build an even stronger academic foundation. My long-term goal is to complete my bachelor's degree in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning in Turkey, and subsequently pursue a master's degree in this field in the United States. I aim to immerse myself in an academic and productive environment that reflects my true potential, allowing me to focus deeply on my primary objectives. Currently, my immediate priorities are expanding my portfolio on GitHub, continuing my research in AI and machine learning, and integrating this technical output with photography to develop a versatile, multifaceted approach to creative production.
